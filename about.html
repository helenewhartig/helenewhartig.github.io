ABOUT HELENE

My busy boutique law firm includes many clients (and their family members) who I have successfully represented in all facets of real estate law. This includes negotiating complex residential and commercial buy outs, assisting in first time cooperative or condominium purchases, collecting arrears from delinquent shareholders, defending (victimized) tenants against frivolous non primary residency or owner occupancy proceedings, researching benefits available to senior citizens and disabled tenants, and assuring that harassment, discrimination, rent overcharge and reduction of services complaints are properly filed and pursued in the appropriate forums.

As a native (and almost senior) New Yorker, I appreciate the anger and angst of appearing in court, and the wide range of emotions involved in landlord tenant litigation. Over the years, as corporate counsel for many cooperatives and condominiums in Manhattan, and as long term president of my own fifty six unit cooperative on the Upper East Side, I have also learned the importance of communicating effectively with real estate brokers, bankers and mortgage brokers, managing agents, contractors, neighbors and building staff. Further, I understand the unique financial and legal challenges faced by "mom and pop" operations; that's because my father owned two drapery stores on the Lower East Side. Often, we worked side by side, enabling me to learn the fine art of negotiations and the value of a dollar. Currently, I am able to apply these principles on a daily basis, by assuring that my work product is on time, on track, and on budget.

Helene W. Hartig, Esq. is the owner and principal of our law firm. Helene graduated from St. John's Law School in 1984, and is admitted to practice in New York and Pennsylvania. She has managed her own firm since 1991, and has interacted successfully with a diverse range of clients, judges, counsel, managing agents, boards of managers and directors, mortgage brokers and title companies. She has been on the forefront of many evolving areas of law including 2nd Avenue Subway issues, sponsor defaults, and owner-occupancy. She is a member of the New York Woman's Bar Association, Jewish Lawyers Guild, New York Council of Cooperatives & Condominiums, and Board of Advisers, Center for Labor and Employment Law (St. John's University). She is active in various standing committees of New York State Bar Association. Also, she is president and a long time board member of a fifty-six unit cooperative on the Upper East Side of Manhattan. Helene also works closely with senior citizens and disabled New Yorkers and has completed the accreditations required to be appointed as a court guardian.
